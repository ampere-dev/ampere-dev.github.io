High-Frequency Power Systems Demand True Resonance Simulation

Modern aerospace, defense, radar, avionics, and resonant power electronics operate at precise frequencies (including 400 Hz aircraft standards and kHz-range converters) where inductive and capacitive reactances must perfectly balance. Even minor mismatches cause instability, overheating, harmonic distortion, voltage/frequency excursions, or catastrophic failure under load.

Standard resistive-only or basic reactive load banks cannot replicate true resonant behavior. How RLC Resonant Load Banks Work

Resistive (R)

Simulates real (active) power. Current passes through precision resistor elements, converting electrical energy directly to heat at unity power factor (PF = 1.0). Voltage and current are perfectly in phase.

Inductive (L)

Wire coils create magnetic fields. Current lags voltage (lagging PF). Used to reduce overall power factor and simulate inductive loads common in motors, transformers, and avionics.

Capacitive (C)

Capacitors store charge and cause current to lead voltage (leading PF). Raises circuit power factor and simulates modern electronics, LED lighting, and resonant converters.

Resonance & Tunable Control

At the resonant frequency, inductive reactance (XL = 2πfL) exactly cancels capacitive reactance (XC = 1/(2πfC)), resulting in minimum impedance and purely resistive behavior. Our tunable RLC systems let engineers dial in exact resonance across a wide frequency range — from 60 Hz utility to 400 Hz aerospace standards and into kHz for wireless power, radar, and resonant DC-DC converters. Independent digital control of R, L & C delivers any power factor from 0.0 leading to 0.0 lagging with laboratory precision, plus real-time metering and SCPI/LabVIEW data logging.
