**Posted by Ampere Development Team**

The Electrical Generating Systems Association (EGSA) has launched a Load Bank Certification course, designed to enhance the skills of professionals in the on-site power generation industry. Detailed on EGSA’s education page (egsa.org/Education/Load-Bank-Certification), this 2.5-day program combines hands-on and classroom training to cover critical topics such as load bank operation, safety protocols, and performance testing. Aimed at technicians and engineers working with power systems, the course concludes with a comprehensive performance and written certification exam, ensuring participants are well-equipped to maintain and optimize load banks in real-world applications.

The course, which includes the latest edition of EGSA’s On-Site Power Generation Reference Book, addresses the growing demand for skilled professionals in an industry where reliable power is essential. As one industry observer noted, “EGSA now offers a ‘Load Bank Certification’ course for those of you that would like to brush up on your skills. I know of some major rental companies that should send its personnel to such classes, but I am not naming names here.” This sentiment underscores the value of EGSA’s program, which offers a competitive edge for technicians seeking to excel. The training emphasizes practical skills, such as troubleshooting and system integration, while reinforcing best practices for compliance with industry standards. By fostering expertise and credibility, EGSA’s Load Bank Certification empowers professionals to enhance operational efficiency and safety in power generation environments.
